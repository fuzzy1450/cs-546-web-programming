All The Maps

Monaco

Ready for some close-quarters combat? Monaco has you covered! The beautiful view of the mediterranian will have you Kicking back, cracking open a cold OSPUZE, and trying to relax. But only for as long as the other teams let you!

Monaco is famous for it's winding corridors, snug living spaces, and surplus of clay shingles. Be wary of people with sledgehammers: they may have too much fun.

  1. The Cathedral
  2. Construction Site
  3. Ruelle des Lilas

Las Vegas

Shine like a star under the glitz and glamour of Vegas' iconic neon lights. Spin some wheels and win some money, but try not to lose yourself in the resulting rubble.

This map is infamous for it's long corridor, giving our contestants an opportunity to flex their marksmanship. For those of you that prefer close-quarters engagements; fear not! Vegas' giant casino complexes will give our more intimate contestants plenty of cover, letting you close distance while avoiding the local sniper-light!

  1. Eastwood Casino
  2. Glamora
  3. Death By Rodent Concert
  4. The Great Sandstorm Of 2032

Seoul

Welcome to humble Seoul, where the ground is no gurantee. In the Korean capital, combat happens 30 stories in the air! Our contestants will be navigating the skyscraper rooftops of this very modern cityscape. The ground is a long way down!

Seoul's spartan design allows our contestants to really show what they're made of! With simple architecture and plenty of breathing room, this locale will have contestants relying on their teammates rather than cover.

  1. The Hospital
  2. The Glass Triangle
  3. Moving Platforms

SYS$HORIZON

And we're back! Welcome to SYS$HORIZON, the latest and greatest arena for when you need a break from meatspace. With it's unique architecture and network of jump pads, contestants will be spending less time traveling and more time fighting over the point.

This map may not behave the way that most contestants expect - many rules are broken by the virtual architecture, especially gravity. Rewriting the rulebook makes for some lovely chaos.

  1. LIBRARY_TEST_0
  2. CAMPUS_AREA
  3. ART_GALLERY_2
  4. The Dolphins

Kyoto (1568)

An old take on a new classic: Kyoto (1568) wwill make contestants nostalgic for a time long past. But be careful: many of the walls are little more than paper, and paper doesnt stop bullets (or hammers).

This map features a beautiful slice of ancient Kyoto. It is filled with elevation changes and steep angles, making it perfect for the jumppad-minded mediums out there.

  2. Central Tower
  3. Any Crawlspace
